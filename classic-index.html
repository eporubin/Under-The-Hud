Meet the people

Hi there, I’m Richard Oastler , I help forward-thinking clients succeed in digital culture.

Born in 1789 Oastler was a commission agent, after completing ten years on his field Richard was nominated as steward for Thomas Thornill, the absentee landlord of fixby, an estate near Huddersfield. In 1830 Oastler became familiar with the currently and very poor structure of the factory legislation and made a mission of his to improve the same, so the people would have a more dignified live. His previous connections with the tory party represented an advantage during this fight. After overcoming great obstacles finally in 1847 Oastler saw his efforts being paid off with the 1847 Factory Act being passed by the parliament. Under such regulation woman and children are not work more then 10h per day and 58 hours a week within the textile industry. In 1867 the Act was applying to all manufacturing businesses within the UK.
