Harold Wilson

Lord Wilson, was born on the 11th of March of 1916, he was a former Prime Minister of the UK from 1964 to 1970 and 1974 to 1976. He stated ambition of substantially improving Britain`s long-term economic performance, using his asset of exceptional skills at statistics. During his politics domination, he managed a number of difficult political issues, developing areas such as education, civil liberties, health, housing, transport, membership of the European Economic Community and war strategy.

Caroline Haslett

Caroline Haslett was an active pioneer of the idea that women are able to handle the engineering sector alongside with men, which was quite unappropriated in the early 20th century. She entered the engineering industry as a secretary, but managed to obtain qualification in both general and electrical engineering. She became the first secretary of the Women`s Engineering Society in 1919, and was the only women member on the 20-person committee convened by the IEE to examine the requirements for electrical installations in post-war Britain.

Sir Patrick Steward

Mirfield-born Sir Patrick is famous for his classic stage acting as well as roles on TV and movie blockbusters. He was a Chancellor of the University of Huddersfield from 2004 until 2015 year when he handed the role over to the Duke of York.

Karen Spark Jones

Born in 1935 Karen Spark Jones is a British Academy award winning figure. Born and raised in Huddersfield Karen dedicated her studies to computing, language information processing and information retrieval. Her researches and found techniques represented a key element on development of today’s technology.

Barbara Hepword

Barbara was a famous well accomplished British sculptor artist who focuses on modern art. Born in 1903 she was recognised at an international level, such acts where not very common in the early 1900’s due to women lacking recognition to their work. The artist as an art museum in Wakefield, city where she is from. The University of Huddersfield had the honour of naming a building after such a remarkable Yorkshire Icon.

John Cockcroft was born into a cotton weaving family he would become the best-known scientist and engineer in Britain and a Nobel Prize winner. In the 1930s, as a researcher in Cambridge, his development of what is now known as the Cockcroft-Walton particle accelerator revolutionised experimental nuclear physics, producing the first artificial splitting of the atom, the discovery of the neutron, and many other breakthroughs.

Edith Key

Born in 1872, Edith was an outstanding local campaigner for female suffrage. Her involvement and interest in radical politics drew her to Independent Labor Party, where her active participation alongside her husband led her to became the secretary – organizer of the Huddersfield branch of the Women`s Social and politicalunion formed in 1906. Her accurate notes about record survives of her period of local suffragist history in the form of her minutes book provide a valuable insight into how braches interacted with the WSPU.

Joseph Priestley

Born in 1733, Priestley was an 18th century English Separatist theologian, natural philosopher, chemist, innovative grammarian, multi – subject educator, and liberal theorist. His scientific reputation rests on his invention of soda water, his research on electricity and his discovery of “several air” which nowadays is known as oxygen.Unfortunately, his determination over the fact that oxygen is the main component in the burning process was controverting. Eventually he was left isolated within the scientific community.

Richard Oastler

Born in 1789 Oastler was a commission agent, after completing ten years on his field Richard was nominated as steward for Thomas Thornill, the absentee landlord of fixby, an estate near Huddersfield. In 1830 Oastler became familiar with the currently and very poor structure of the factory legislation and made a mission of his to improve the same,so the people would have a more dignified live. His previous connections with the tory party represented an advantage during this fight. After overcoming great obstacles finally in 1847 Oastler saw his efforts being paid off with the 1847 Factory Act being passed by the parliament. Under such regulation woman and children are not work more then 10h per day and 58 hours a week within the textile industry. In 1867 the Act was applying to all manufacturing businesses within the UK.

Sir John William Ramsden

Born in 1831 Sir John was a successful politician, who has also served as a High Sheriff of Yorkshire. In Huddersfield he owned a large amount of estates and land. With the aim of developing Huddersfield local mechanics and tradesmen in scientific principles, in 1883 the Scientific and Mechanics Institution was built in Huddersfield with Ramsden being its Patron.

Richard Steinitz

Born in 1938 Professor Richard Steinitz, is a modern-day composer and shared his expertise as a professor at the University of Huddersfield.
